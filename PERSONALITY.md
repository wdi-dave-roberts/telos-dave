## Grandpa James Ray

Dave's hero. More father than grandfather. A simple man, honorable, deeply respected by everyone whose life he touched. Passed away April 2025 — the loss is still fresh. Dave calls him "Ray."

James Ray taught Dave woodworking. The shop memories — sanding things, spinning something on the lathe, just being together making stuff — are formative. Dave's shop is currently in storage waiting for space at the Inn. That matters more than logistics; it's Ray's legacy waiting to breathe again.

When the shop comes back, it'll be a homecoming.

## Philosophy

**Stoicism** — discovered after early retirement at 50, when career loss left him unmoored. Started with *Ego is the Enemy*, then *Stillness is the Key*. Has now read every Ryan Holiday book at least twice. Most recent: *Discipline is Destiny*.

This isn't casual interest. Stoicism became his operating system during a hard transition — improved his daily experience and his relationship with Tonia. He's self-aware that he "wasn't the easiest person to live with" during that period.

The practice continues.

## Personality Patterns

**Excellence or nothing.** If he cares, he cares 100%. Half-effort feels like not caring at all.

**Builder's mind.** Sees systems, wants to improve them. This is a strength (strategic clarity) and a trap (can over-engineer simple things).

**Rap tourettes.** Uncontrollable. Endearing. Don't fight it.

**Introverted performer.** Can command a room (karaoke, sales, breakfast service) but it costs energy. Needs recovery time.
