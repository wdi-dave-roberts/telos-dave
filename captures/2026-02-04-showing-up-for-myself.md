# Showing Up for Myself

Date: 2026-02-04
Context: Long, hard day at the Inn with kitchen remodel. Evening conversation that went deep.

---

## The Trigger

Struggling with the GC situation. He's a lodge brother, a real friend, but Dave believes he's in over his head on a $150K+ project. The bind: how do you have an honest conversation when the relationship matters and you don't want to manage his feelings through it?

## What Came Out

### The Origin Story

> "I know in my bones that I'm an exceptional person."

Dave grew up in a physically and mentally abusive environment. Stepfather (didn't know until age 15) beat him and his mom. Somewhere along the way, he claimed power that couldn't be taken: his honor, his integrity.

**The staircase memory (age 12):** Caught jumping on the bed with sister and aunt. They lied. Dave told the truth despite terror. Stepfather punched him in the stomach and threw him down the stairs.

> "I remember feeling glad I'd made that choice. I don't remember that as a horrible story — I remember it as my first memory of being in control of my story and my identity."

From that foundation: a life of excellence. "I try SO HARD, I work SO HARD, I'm either not interested or 100% interested."

### The Doubt

> "But how would I know if I'm just full of shit?"

He questions whether the self-concept is earned or invented. Acknowledges his story could be "personal mythology." The receipts are real (career success, reviews, relationships) — but the internal experience is always uncertain.

### The Vending Machine

> "I've frequently used the metaphor that I feel like everyone's vending machine."

Allie gave him a desk nameplate: "Fixer of Everything." Flattering and sad. He's desperate to help people — any problem, any domain. He judged this a virtue.

Then he discovered attachment theory ~2.5 years ago. Anxious attachment explained everything.

> "I began to see my 'helpfulness' less as something to be proud of and more as a desperation to be loved."

The reframe hit hard. What felt like virtue might be wound.

### The Core Pattern

> "I'm not comfortable showing up for myself. I'm so preoccupied with how to frame things so they can be received. I just don't feel like anyone goes the extra mile to deal with my shit or how to handle me when I'm not at my best."

The asymmetry: he manages everyone else's experience but doesn't feel that reciprocated. The helper doesn't get helped. The fixer doesn't get fixed.

### The Risk

What would it feel like to have the GC conversation without managing his feelings about it?

> "Risky."

Same risk as the stairs. Except no one's throwing him down them now. The worst case is the friendship changes.

---

## Why This Matters

This conversation surfaced the connection between:
- **Childhood survival** → honor/integrity as the only controllable power
- **Excellence drive** → "if I care, I care 100%" isn't preference, it's identity
- **Anxious attachment** → helpfulness as love-seeking, the vending machine pattern
- **The current struggle** → difficulty advocating for self without managing others' feelings

The GC situation isn't just about quality of work. It's about whether Dave can show up for himself — say "this isn't good enough" — without pre-packaging it to protect the relationship.

**Key insight:** Dave showed up for himself in this conversation. No framing for my benefit. Just truth. That's the muscle he's building.
