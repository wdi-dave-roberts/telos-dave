# BELIEFS

> *What I hold to be true about the world*

---

## It All Starts With Safety

The Hierarchy of Care (Safety → Comfort → Connection → Cuisine → Beauty → Captivation) isn't a business framework I designed for the Inn. It's my personal ethic that I discovered while building something for guests. Safety first isn't a hospitality decision — it's how I'm wired.

This shows up everywhere:
- In software: security and accessibility before features
- In relationships: trust and physical/emotional safety before intimacy
- In leadership: stability before ambition
- In AI: privacy architecture before offering someone a personal thinking space
- At the Inn: clean, safe, secure before beautiful or captivating

**The Hierarchy of Care is Dave's ethics, externalized.**

But — and this matters — the Inn's version of it needs Tonia's fingerprints too. I built it alone, from my perspective. Her ethics might reorder things, add things, emphasize differently. Connection might be higher for her. Cuisine isn't just a tier — it's her language of love. For the Hierarchy of Care to be truly *lived* at White Doe, it needs both our signatures on it. Right now it has mine. That's incomplete.

---

## Excellence for Its Own Sake

Not for reputation. Not for reviews. Because doing something well is the only way I know how to care. Half-effort is the same as not caring.

## The Unseen Details Matter Most

The Bear, Forks episode. The things no one will ever notice are the things that define who you actually are. If you wouldn't be proud of the work with the lights on, don't do it in the dark.
