# CHALLENGES

> *What I'm working through*

---

## The Resistance

I avoid guest interactions — breakfast service, happy hour — despite knowing they fuel me, ground me in my mission, and reinforce that I'm in the right place. When I feel their reactions, I feel purpose.

But sometimes I just don't go. It's not that something more important is happening. I just don't want to show up. Against my ethics, against the ideals I've set for myself, and I know it when I'm doing it.

It's not fear. Fear stops you from doing something you want. This is different. It's resistance — and I don't know what it's protecting me from.

The keyboard feels safe. The dining room asks something of me that I can't name yet.

**Status:** Open question. Living with it.

---

## Tonia's Fingerprints

I built the Hierarchy of Care alone. I want it to carry both our signatures, but I'm afraid she can't meet me there. Her strength is practical, not philosophical. Her wounds from childhood make her avoid rather than examine. She hasn't changed much in how she understands herself since we were kids.

I love her completely. And I hold this truth about her gently. It doesn't diminish what she brings — her hospitality instinct is pure and unreplicable. But the frameworks, the articulation of *why* we do what we do — that's mine alone, and it makes the Hierarchy incomplete.

**Status:** Unresolved. May not be resolvable in the way I want.

---

## The Fear of Poverty

Raised in poverty. Hyper-protected our means my entire adult life. Conservative investments, house beneath our means, never overspent. But investing in *myself* — believing I could succeed at something on my own terms — I couldn't get there.

Psilocybin removed the fear. Not the caution — the *paralysis*. My engineer brain woke up and proved the Inn was feasible. Without the fear, I could act.

The fear is mostly gone now. But the wiring is still there. I still notice it.

**Status:** Largely resolved. Watch for regression under financial stress.

---

## The Feelings Dynamic

Frequent and intense fighting over feelings — specifically around her "not allowing me to have feelings." This has been the recurring conflict throughout our relationship, intensifying over the past couple of years.

I'm starting to see my part in this more clearly. She seems to be seeing hers. Whether the dynamic that's existed our entire relationship can truly change is an open question, but there's cautious optimism.

**Status:** Active. Both sides recognizing their contributions. Watch for regression into old patterns.

---

## Overcomplication

I tend to overcomplicate things — wedding prep, systems, processes — when simpler approaches would serve just as well or better. Serious diminishing returns. This connects to the efficiency instinct: I optimize when I should just do.

**Status:** Recognized. Actively watching for it.
